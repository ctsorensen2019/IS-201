Experience of Growth

Recently, I had to decide if I wanted to stay in Utah or go back home to Georgia for the summer. It was a hard decision, because I have a girlfriend and good job here. On the other side, it would be much cheaper to live at home. It seemed as though there was no "right" answer after much deliberation and prayer. The hard thing was not wanting to displease anyone, but whatever place I chose to live would make someone unhappy. In the end I decided to stay in Utah, and everything started to click into place. My boss gave me a promotion and a pay raise when I decided to stay, and none of the internships I applied for in Georgia ended up working out. It helped me reiterate the path to finding an answer and making a hard decision when I feel confused.
